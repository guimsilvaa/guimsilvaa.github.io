I'm honored to have co-participated as a co-author of the poster "Computational Insights into Dual-specificity Tyrosine Phosphorylation-regulated Kinase 1A (DYRK1A) Selectivity" presentated at the OpenEye CUP XXV (Santa Fe, NM, March 10–12, 2026).

Of course, I'm extremely grateful to [Ariadna Llop-Peiro](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ariadnallop) for the opportunity and for continuing this work that I started during my Postdoc. Thank you so much, Ariadna! 

Also thanks to [Dr. Lijun Sun](https://research.bidmc.org/lijun-sun). Very exciting to see this work's on progress!

In short: we explored the structural basis of selectivity in DYRK1A inhibitors—an ongoing challenge due to the conserved ATP-binding pocket—using docking and molecular dynamics simulations to compare interactions with DYRK1A and CLK1. Our results show that the lack of key bidentate hydrogen bonds and reduced hydrophobic pocket occupation in CLK1 likely explain the observed selectivity, offering clear directions for rational design of improved inhibitors.
