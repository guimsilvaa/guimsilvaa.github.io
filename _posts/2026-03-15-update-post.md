I'm honored to have virtually participated as a coauthor of this poster presentation at the OpenEye CUP XXV (Santa Fe, NM, March 10–12, 2026).

Of course, I'm extremely grateful to Ariadna Llop-Peiro for the opportunity to contribute and to co-present this work. Thank you so much, Ariadna! Also thanks to Dr. Lijun Sun :)

We explored the structural basis of selectivity in DYRK1A inhibitors—an ongoing challenge due to the conserved ATP-binding pocket—using docking and molecular dynamics simulations to compare interactions with DYRK1A and CLK1. Our results show that the lack of key bidentate hydrogen bonds and reduced hydrophobic pocket occupation in CLK1 likely explain the observed selectivity, offering clear directions for rational design of improved inhibitors.

Very excited to see this work's on progress.
